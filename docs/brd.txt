Confidential — Internal Use Only  Page 2 1. Scope  
The Pricing Intelligence Agent is designed as an autonomous AI -driven system that supports pricing and commercial teams in 
transforming client RFPs into accurate, structured, and approval -ready pricing proposals. The current process relies heavily on 
manua l effort for interpreting RFPs, extracting requirements, mapping costs, and preparing pricing documents. This leads to 
inconsistencies, delays, and limited transparency in pricing decisions. The AI agent will act as a centralized intelligence l ayer 
that au tomates data extraction, pricing computation, benchmarking, and documentation, ensuring faster turnaround time and 
improved accuracy across the pricing lifecycle.  
The scope includes intelligent ingestion and analysis of RFP documents. The agent will process documents in multiple formats 
and extract key inputs such as pricing model, service types, language pairs, regional variants, and volume estimates. It will  
stand ardize extracted data into structured formats such as normalized locale codes and volume scenarios. In cases of 
incomplete or ambiguous data, the agent will apply intelligent assumptions such as low, medium, and high scenarios, while 
flagging missing or un supported inputs for user validation.  
The scope also includes automated pricing computation and decision intelligence. The agent will integrate with internal cost 
databases to retrieve buy rates for each service type and locale. It will apply user -defined inputs such as project management 
overhead and target margin to compute sell rates. A transparent cost -to-price bridge will be generated for each pricing output, 
ensuring full visibility into pricing logic. The agent will also perform competitive benchmarking using historical rate datab ases 
and provide pricing recommendations based on past deal performance.  
The solution includes workflow orchestration with mandatory Human -in-Loop checkpoints. The agent will enforce user inputs 
for margin and overhead, and require approval before proceeding to template population. It will ensure that all pricing outpu ts 
are va lidated before finalization. Once approved, the agent will automatically populate client pricing templates with rates, total 
contract values, and structured pricing outputs. It will also generate commercial assumptions including service levels, tax 
treatme nt, validity periods, and volume tolerance clauses.  
The scope further includes strategic insights and contract risk analysis. The agent will generate internal pricing strategy 
summaries for leadership review, including deal value, margin insights, and competitive positioning. It will analyze contract s 
again st predefined approval matrices, identify risk areas, classify risk levels, and recommend escalation paths. This ensures 
compliance with internal policies and improves decision -making at leadership levels.  
Additionally, the solution includes end -to-end automation, monitoring, and auditability. The agent will maintain detailed logs of 
all actions, including data extraction, pricing calculations, approvals, and outputs. It will ensure full traceability and 
transparency across the pricing lifecycle. The system will operate on a scalable and event -driven architecture, supporting 
multiple RFPs and large datasets simultaneously. It will produce structured deliverables such as pricing matrices, 
benchmarking reports,  populated templates, commercial documents, and risk analysis outputs, thereby reducing manual effort 
and improving operational efficiency.  
The scope also includes extensibility and continuous improvement. The agent will learn from historical pricing decisions, use r 
inputs, and deal outcomes to improve future recommendations. It will support configurable rules, dynamic pricing strategies, 
and integration with additional enterprise systems. It will enable organizations to evolve pricing strategies over time while 
maintaining consistency, governance, and scalability.

3.1 Human -in-Loop 
• The Pricing Intelligence Agent operates with a controlled Human -in-Loop model to ensure accuracy, compliance, and 
business validation at critical stages of the pricing lifecycle. While the agent automates data extraction, pricing 
computation, benchmarking,  and document generation, human intervention is required at key decision points to 
maintain governance and accountability.  
• The first level of Human -in-Loop interaction occurs during data validation and assumption confirmation . Pricing 
analysts review extracted RFP inputs such as pricing model, service types, language pairs, and volume scenarios. 
Business Requirements Document — Pricing Intelligence Agent    |   CTA Department   |   V1   |   07/04/2026  
Confidential — Internal Use Only  Page 8 Any missing or ambiguous data identified by the agent must be validated or corrected by the user before further 
processing. This ensures that all downstream pricing calculations are based on accurate and approved inputs.  
• The second level of intervention is during pricing computation and margin validation . The agent computes pricing 
using cost data, overheads, and margin inputs. However, users are required to review and confirm these inputs, 
especially for high -value deals or strategic opportunities. Analysts can adjust margins, apply overrides, or modify 
assumptions based on business context before approving the pricing output.  
• The third level of Human -in-Loop control is applied during benchmarking and recommendation validation . The 
agent provides pricing recommendations based on historical data and market positioning. Users must review these 
recommendations and validate whether the suggested pricing aligns with business strategy, competitiveness, and 
risk appetite. This ensures  that automated recommendations are aligned with real -world decision -making.  
• The fourth level of intervention is during final approval and template generation . The agent does not proceed to 
populate client -facing pricing templates unless explicit approval is provided by the pricing team or manager. This step 
ensures that all pricing outputs, contract values, and commercial assumptions are reviewed and approved before 
being shared externally.  
• The fifth level of Human -in-Loop involvement is in contract risk analysis and escalation handling . The agent 
identifies risks in commercial clauses and classifies them based on predefined rules. High -risk scenarios require 
manual review and approval from pricing managers or leadership. Users may decide to accept, mitigate, or escalate 
risks based on b usiness priorities.  
• Additionally, Human -in-Loop support is required for exception handling . Scenarios such as missing cost data, low -
confidence benchmarking, unsupported locales, or inconsistent inputs will trigger alerts. These cases require user 
intervention to resolve issues before the workflow can continue.  
• Overall, the Human -in-Loop model ensures that the Pricing Intelligence Agent operates with a balance of automation 
and human control. It enables efficiency while maintaining accuracy, compliance, and strategic decision -making 
across the pricing lifecycle.

workflow  
5 Standard formats such as locale codes (for example BCP -47) are defined and available for data normalization and 
processing
